🌍 15 DELEGATIONS WITH FULL BRIEFS
🇺🇸 United States

Representative: Ambassador Sarah Whitman

The United States prioritizes regional stability, protection of allies (especially Israel), and preventing nuclear proliferation. Publicly, it frames its involvement as defensive and aimed at deterring Iranian aggression. Its red lines include Iranian nuclear weaponization, attacks on U.S. personnel, and threats to maritime security in the Gulf. Washington strongly supports UN resolutions condemning Iran’s actions and advocates sanctions enforcement .

Privately, the U.S. is willing to tolerate limited Iranian regional influence if it guarantees de-escalation and nuclear compliance. It may also accept indirect negotiations via intermediaries. Key relationships include strong alignment with Israel and NATO allies, cautious coordination with Gulf states, and tense rivalry with Russia and China.

🇮🇷 Iran

Representative: Ambassador Amir Hosseini

Iran prioritizes regime survival, regional influence, and resistance against Western intervention. Publicly, it frames itself as a victim of aggression and defender of sovereignty. Its red lines include foreign military presence near its borders and threats to its nuclear program. Iran relies on a network of regional allies such as Hezbollah and other militias .

Privately, Iran may accept sanctions relief and security guarantees in exchange for limiting escalation. It seeks recognition as a regional power. Key relationships include strategic ties with Russia and China, and adversarial relations with the U.S., Israel, and several Gulf states.

🇮🇱 Israel

Representative: Ambassador Daniel Regev

Israel’s priority is preventing existential threats, especially Iran’s nuclear capabilities and proxy networks. Publicly, it defends preemptive strikes as necessary self-defense. Red lines include Iranian nuclear development and weapons transfers to Hezbollah.

Privately, Israel may accept de-escalation if Iran’s military capabilities are verifiably reduced. It relies heavily on U.S. support and coordination. Key relationships include strong ties with the U.S. and improving ties with some Arab states, while remaining in direct conflict with Iran.

🇷🇺 Russia

Representative: Ambassador Sergei Volkov

Russia prioritizes maintaining influence in the Middle East and countering U.S. dominance. Publicly, it calls for diplomacy and criticizes Western intervention.

Privately, Russia benefits from instability that distracts the West but avoids direct escalation. It supports Iran politically but stops short of full military backing .

Key relationships include cooperation with Iran and China, while maintaining pragmatic ties with Israel.

🇨🇳 China

Representative: Ambassador Liu Wen

China emphasizes sovereignty, non-interference, and economic stability. Publicly, it calls for ceasefire and negotiation.

Privately, it seeks to secure energy supplies and expand influence through mediation. It may tolerate Iran’s regional role if trade routes remain stable.

Key relationships include strong economic ties with Iran and cautious neutrality toward Western powers.

🇫🇷 France

Representative: Ambassador Claire Dubois

France prioritizes multilateralism and nuclear non-proliferation. Publicly, it supports UN-led solutions and condemns escalation.

Privately, France may support limited intervention if justified legally. It seeks a diplomatic leadership role in Europe.

Key relationships include EU partners and coordination with the U.S., while maintaining dialogue with Iran.

🇬🇧 United Kingdom

Representative: Ambassador James Carter

The UK aligns closely with the U.S. on security but emphasizes international law. Publicly, it condemns Iranian aggression.

Privately, it prefers diplomatic containment over full-scale war.

Key relationships include NATO allies and Gulf partnerships.

🇸🇦 Saudi Arabia

Representative: Ambassador Faisal Al Saud

Saudi Arabia prioritizes regional security and countering Iranian influence. Publicly, it condemns Iran’s actions.

Privately, it fears escalation and may accept backchannel diplomacy.

Key relationships include the U.S. and Gulf Cooperation Council states.

🇦🇪 United Arab Emirates

Representative: Ambassador Noura Al Mazrouei

The UAE focuses on economic stability and regional security. Publicly, it supports de-escalation.

Privately, it balances between opposing Iran and maintaining trade links.

Key relationships include the U.S., Saudi Arabia, and pragmatic ties with Iran.

🇹🇷 Turkey

Representative: Ambassador Mehmet Kaya

Turkey seeks regional influence and strategic autonomy. Publicly, it calls for mediation.

Privately, it may leverage the crisis for geopolitical gain.

Key relationships include NATO, Russia, and Middle Eastern actors.

🇮🇳 India

Representative: Ambassador Rajiv Mehta

India prioritizes energy security and neutrality. Publicly, it calls for peace.

Privately, it seeks stable access to Iranian oil.

Key relationships include the U.S., Iran, and Gulf states.

🇧🇷 Brazil

Representative: Ambassador Ana Ribeiro

Brazil emphasizes diplomacy and Global South leadership. Publicly, it opposes escalation.

Privately, it seeks a mediator role.

Key relationships include BRICS and non-aligned states.

🇩🇪 Germany

Representative: Ambassador Lukas Schneider

Germany prioritizes stability and EU unity. Publicly, it supports sanctions and diplomacy.

Privately, it fears economic fallout.

Key relationships include EU and NATO.

🇿🇦 South Africa

Representative: Ambassador Thandi Nkosi

South Africa promotes non-alignment and peace. Publicly, it criticizes Western intervention.

Privately, it seeks BRICS solidarity.

Key relationships include China, Russia, and Global South.

🇯🇵 Japan

Representative: Ambassador Hiroshi Tanaka

Japan prioritizes energy security and alliance with the U.S. Publicly, it calls for restraint.

Privately, it seeks uninterrupted oil supply.

Key relationships include the U.S. and Middle East partners.
